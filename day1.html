Chest, Shoulders, Triceps and Abs Day

Toji Fushiguro is the kind of character who walks into a room and instantly commands respect, everyone can tell this guy is built differently. The main reason? Four perfectly developed and balanced muscle groups: the shoulders, chest, triceps, and abs.

Shoulders (Deltoids) Wide, round, cannonball delts create the classic V-taper: broad up top, narrow at the waist. This is the single most important feature for looking strong and athletic even with clothes on. Big shoulders improve posture, make the arms look bigger, and give that intimidating “I own this space” silhouette.

Chest (Pectorals) A thick, square chest fills out any shirt and creates the armored, bulletproof look. It’s the centerpiece of the upper body. When the pecs are fully developed (especially the upper chest), the whole torso looks taller, stronger, and more masculine.

The triceps make up roughly ⅔ of your arm size. Horseshoe-shaped, defined triceps are what make arms look massive and powerful from every angle, not just when you flex the biceps. They’re the difference between “decent arms” and “those arms could knock someone out.”

Abs & Core Even though they’re usually hidden under a shirt in Toji’s case, a rock-solid, tight midsection pulls everything together. It keeps the waist small, accentuates the V-taper, improves posture, and shows that the person has discipline and low enough body fat to reveal real functional strength.

Chest, Shoulders and Triceps exercises

Barbell Bench Press (4×6–8)
Overhead Shoulder Press (4×6–8)
Incline Dumbbell Press (3×8–10)
Lateral Raises (3×12–15)
Tricep Rope Pushdown (3×10–12)
Dips (2×failure)

Abs exercises

Hanging Leg Raises (3×10–15)
Plank (2×60 sec)
