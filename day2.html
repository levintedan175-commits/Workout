Forearms,Back and Biceps Day

Toji doesn’t just look strong from the front — turn him sideways or watch him from behind and the message is even clearer: this man is a walking weapon. The reason? An insanely thick, wide back paired with peaked biceps and rope-like forearms that look forged for ripping things (and people) apart. When the pull muscles are fully developed, you get that rare “cobra” look: massive lats that flare out like wings, a thick upper back that makes the waist appear tiny, and arms that look dangerous even when relaxed.

Back (Lats, Traps, Rhomboids, Rear Delts) A wide, thick back is the foundation of the X-frame physique. Huge lats create that dramatic V-taper from behind, while tall traps and rhomboids give the “yoked” look that screams raw power. Even in a loose shirt, a developed back makes you look broader and more imposing. Functionally, it’s what lets Toji pull, climb, swing weapons, and dominate grappling situations with zero effort.

Peak biceps are the ultimate flex symbol. When Toji’s arms hang at his sides, you can already see the split and height — that’s genetic freak-level insertion + years of heavy pulling. Big biceps aren’t just for show: they’re the primary muscle for pulling, curling, and crushing grips. They turn every arm into a loaded weapon.

Forearms Most people neglect forearms. Toji clearly didn’t. Thick, veiny forearms with dense flexor and extensor muscles are what separate “gym arms” from “killer arms.” They make grip strength monstrous (perfect for wielding cursed tools or choking out opponents), improve wrist stability, and give that brutal, battle-scarred look even when the sleeves are down.

Back and Biceps exercises

Pull-ups (4x6-10)
Barbell Rows (4x6-8)
Lat Pulldown (3x10-12)
Face Pulls (3x12-15)
Barbell Curls (3x8-10)
Hammer Curls (3x10)

Forearms exercises

Reverse Curls (3x12)
Wrist Curls (3x15)
